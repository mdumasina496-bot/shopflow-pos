Logging In

Clocking In for Attendance

Before you start selling, clock in for the day. Go to Admin Menu and tap Attendance. Enter your password to record that you have arrived. Do this every single shift — at the start and at the end.

Let's process a takeaway order. First, select your order type. For a customer walking in to buy meat, choose Takeaway.

As you add items, the total updates automatically at the bottom of the cart. When the customer is ready to pay, press the green Charge button showing the total amount.

Action: Press Complete. Show the receipt screen.

👔 Manager Training Video Script

Read the narration naturally — follow screen actions before speaking each section.

Role: Manager Total Time: ~8 minutes Scenes: 10 Demo login: username — butchery_manager / password — 1234
Before recording: Log in as butchery_manager. Have the demo data loaded (it seeds automatically on first load).
1
Introduction
0:00 – 0:30
Show: Manager dashboard with tiles visible.

Welcome to the ShopFlow POS Manager Training guide. This video covers everything you need to run daily floor operations — from opening the shift and assigning cashiers, to processing the end-of-day cashup, receiving stock, and pulling daily reports.

As a manager, you have access to more of the system than cashiers, and your password is needed to approve important actions like voids and cashups. Let's walk through it all.

2
Start of Day — Assigning Cashiers to Tills
0:30 – 1:30
Action: From dashboard → Admin Menu → Assign.

The first thing you do every morning is assign your cashiers to their tills. This tells the system which cashier is working at which till, and how much float — starting cash — is in that till.

Go to Admin Menu and tap Assign.

Action: Enter manager password to unlock Assign.

Enter your manager password to open the assign screen.

Action: Select a cashier from the dropdown, select a till, enter a float amount, and press Assign.

Select the cashier's name, choose their till, and enter the float amount — this is the cash you are putting in the till to start the day with, for giving change. Then press Assign.

The cashier is now linked to their till. Repeat this for every cashier working today. Until you do this, a cashier will see a locked screen when they try to open Orders — so this must be done first.

If you have multiple cashiers, assign them one by one before the shop opens.
3
Approving Voids During the Day
1:30 – 2:15
Show: Navigate to Orders and demonstrate a void being requested.

During the day, cashiers may need to remove an item from a sale — for example, if they scanned the wrong product or the customer changed their mind. This is called a void.

When a cashier presses VOID on an item, they will call you over. You will see a screen showing the item, and a dropdown where the cashier has already selected the reason.

Action: Enter the manager password in the void screen and confirm.

Enter your manager password to approve. The item is then removed from the sale and the full record — the item, amount, reason, cashier, and your name — is saved in the system. You can review all voids later in the Reports section.

Always check that the reason makes sense before approving. If something doesn't look right, ask the cashier to explain before you enter your password.

4
Receiving Stock — GRV
2:15 – 3:15
Action: Navigate to GRV from dashboard. Show the GRV list screen.

When stock arrives from a supplier, you must record it in the system immediately. This is done through the GRV module — Goods Received Voucher.

Go to GRV and press New GRV.

Action: Fill in supplier name, date. Then add an item — select product, enter quantity and cost price.

Enter the supplier name and the delivery date. Then add each item you received — select the product from the list, enter the quantity delivered, and the cost price per unit as shown on the supplier invoice.

Action: Add a second item to show the process. Then press Submit GRV.

Add all items from the delivery. When done, press Submit GRV and Update Stock. The system will instantly update the stock levels and the cost prices for all those products.

Action: Switch to the Charcoal/Coal tab. Show the receive entry form.

If you also received charcoal or coal for the braai, tap the Charcoal tab and record the bags received — including the supplier and cost. Then each day you braai, record how many bags were used under the "Used" entry. This prevents coal going missing without a record.

5
Managing Online Orders
3:15 – 4:00
Action: Go to Online Orders from dashboard. Show the badge count if present.

The Online Orders module is where you manage orders that come in from WhatsApp or phone calls. If there is a red number badge on the tile, that means new orders are waiting.

Tap the tile to open it. New orders will trigger an audio alarm so you always know when one comes in.

Action: Show an order. Tap Start Processing.

When you see a new order, tap Start Processing to let the team know it is being prepared. You can also tap Reply on WhatsApp to send the customer an automatic confirmation message with their estimated wait time.

Action: Navigate to Settings tab. Adjust the wait time slider.

In the Settings tab, you can adjust the waiting time based on how busy the shop is. If you are rushed, set it to 45 or 60 minutes so customers have the right expectation. When it is quiet, bring it back down to 15 or 20 minutes.

6
End-of-Shift Cashup
4:00 – 5:20
Action: Admin Menu → Store Cashup → + New Cashup.

At the end of every shift, you need to do a cashup for each cashier. This is how you confirm that the cash in the till matches what the system says should be there.

Go to Admin Menu, then Store Cashup, and press New Cashup.

Action: Select a cashier/till from the dropdown.

Select the cashier and till you are cashing up. The system will warn you if that cashier has any abandoned orders — orders that were started but never completed. If you see this warning, you must investigate before continuing.

Action: Enter card total and petty cash. Show the denomination grid.

Enter the card machine total from the day, and any petty cash that was used from the till for expenses. Then hand the grid to the cashier.

The cashier counts the physical cash in the till — note and coin by note and coin — and enters the quantities in the grid. Importantly, they cannot see what the system expects. This is called a blind count, and it prevents the cashier from just entering the "right" number without actually counting.

Action: Press Submit for Verification. Enter manager password.

Once the cashier has finished counting, press Submit for Verification. Enter your manager password to confirm.

Show: The variance result on screen.

The system now compares the counted cash to what it expected. If the variance is zero — perfect, the till balances. If it is negative, the cashier is short on cash. If it is positive, there is more cash than expected. Either way, you must investigate before dismissing the cashier.

⏸ PAUSE — let the cashup result show for 3 seconds.
7
Stock Count
5:20 – 6:20
Action: Navigate to Stock Count → Start New Count.

Stock counts should be done regularly — at least once a week — to make sure the physical stock on your shelves matches what the system shows.

Go to Stock Count and press Start New Count. Choose the store and date.

Show: The count entry table — note that System Qty and Variance columns are absent. Only the Count Qty input is shown.

You will notice that the system quantity is hidden during counting. Staff only see the product name and a field to enter what they physically counted. This blind count prevents anyone from just copying the system number instead of actually counting.

Action: Enter a few quantities. Press Submit Count → Apply and Save.

Once counting is complete, press Submit Count. Choose Apply and Save to update the stock levels to the counted quantities. The system will then calculate the variances and show them in the report.

Only the owner can delete a stock count or see the full variance analysis during review.

8
Wastage Log
6:20 – 6:50
Action: Navigate to Wastage Log. Show adding a wastage entry.

Whenever stock is spoiled, dropped, or written off for any reason, it must be recorded in the Wastage Log immediately. Go to Wastage Log, add the product, the quantity wasted, and the reason.

The system will deduct this from stock automatically. If you don't record wastage, your stock counts will always show shortages — and it can look like stock is going missing when it actually just spoiled.

9
Daily Reports to Check
6:50 – 7:40
Action: Navigate to Reports → Order Reports. Show the sub-tabs.

At the end of each day, there are a few key reports you should check. Go to Reports and then Order Reports.

Action: Click Daily Sales tab. Show the bar chart.

Daily Sales shows today's total revenue and number of transactions at a glance.

Action: Click Voids / Overrings tab.

The Voids report shows every item that was voided today — who did it, how much it was for, and the reason. Review this every day. Frequent voids by the same cashier is a red flag.

Action: Click Best Sellers tab.

Best Sellers shows your top-selling products ranked by revenue. This helps you plan what to order more of.

Action: Click Restock Suggestions tab. Show the urgency badges.

And the Restock Suggestions tab is very powerful. It looks at how fast each product is selling and compares it to current stock levels. Products flagged in red are critical — they will run out within three days. Act on these immediately.

10
Closing — Summary
7:40 – 8:00
Show: Manager dashboard.

That covers everything for daily manager operations on ShopFlow POS. Assign cashiers first thing, approve voids during the day, receive stock when deliveries arrive, run cashup at shift end, and check your reports before you leave.

If you ever need a refresher, the Help and Training Guide in the app covers every step in detail. Thank you for watching.

🏪 Owner Training Video Script

Read the narration naturally — follow screen actions before speaking each section.

Role: Owner Total Time: ~10 minutes Scenes: 10 Demo login: username — owner / password — 1234
Before recording: Log in as owner. You will see all tiles — this is the most complete view of the system.
1
Introduction
0:00 – 0:35
Show: Owner dashboard with all tiles visible.

Welcome to the ShopFlow POS Owner Training guide. As the owner, you have full access to every part of the system. This video covers the most important owner-level features — setting up the system, managing your staff, monitoring activity, reading your financial reports, and running butchery operations.

You will notice that your dashboard shows more tiles than managers and cashiers — that is because you have the highest level of access. Let's go through everything you need to know.

2
First-Time Setup — Store Settings
0:35 – 1:20
Action: Admin Menu → Settings → Store Info tab.

The first thing to do when setting up the system is go to Admin Menu, then Settings. Under the Store Info tab, fill in your store name, physical address, phone number, and VAT number if you are VAT registered.

Action: Show the WhatsApp Number field and type a number.

Also enter your WhatsApp business number here — this is the number customers will use to place orders. Enter it in international format with the country code: for South Africa that is 27, followed by the number without the leading zero. For example, 27821234567.

Action: Switch to POS Settings tab. Show the toggles.

In the POS Settings tab, you can turn features on or off for your business — like braai and chisa nyama mode, whether cashiers need to be assigned to a till before selling, and whether split payments are allowed.

Action: Switch to Tills tab. Show the add till form.

Under Tills, set up each till in your store with a name and store. Each till is then available for managers to assign cashiers to.

3
Managing Staff — Users & Access Control
1:20 – 2:30
Action: Navigate to Users from dashboard.

The Users module is where you manage all staff accounts. Only you — as the owner — can access this. Go to Users from your dashboard.

Action: Press + Add User. Fill in name, username, password, role (cashier), store (butchery).

To add a new staff member, press Add User. Fill in their full name, a username they will use to log in, and a password. Set their role — owner, manager, or cashier — and which store they work in: butchery, bottle store, or both.

Show: The Module Access checkbox grid at the bottom of the form.

At the bottom you will see the Module Access section. By default, if you leave all boxes unticked, the staff member has access to everything their role normally allows. But if you want to restrict a specific person — for example, a cashier who should only use the Orders module and Attendance — you can tick only those boxes.

This gives you precise control over who sees what in the system. A cashier who cannot access Inventory cannot adjust stock levels. A manager who cannot access Reports cannot see financial data.

Action: Show the Disable button on an existing user row.

When a staff member leaves, press Disable on their account. Their history stays in the system for records — they just can no longer log in. To change someone's password, press Edit and enter a new password.

Never share your owner password. You are the only person who should access Users, Activity Monitor, and FC Report.
4
Activity Monitor — Watching Everything
2:30 – 3:30
Action: Admin Menu → Activity Monitor.

The Activity Monitor is one of the most powerful tools available to you as the owner. It records every significant action taken in the system — every login, every sale completed, every item voided, every GRV submitted, every stock count, every manager override.

Go to Admin Menu and tap Activity Monitor. This is owner-only — your managers and cashiers cannot see this.

Show: The log entries. Point out the timestamp, staff name, role, and action columns.

Every entry shows the exact time, the staff member's name and role, and what they did. You can see sales being completed, voids being approved, stock being received, and more.

Action: Use the staff member filter and the date filter to narrow results.

Use the filters to investigate a specific person, a specific action type, or a specific date range. For example, if you want to see everything a particular cashier did last Tuesday, select their name and the date — and you have the full picture.

Show: An "Orders Override" entry if present — or explain it.

Pay special attention to entries labelled "Orders Override". This means a manager used their password to access the Orders screen on behalf of a cashier. This should be rare — if you see it happening often, investigate why cashiers are not using their own passwords.

5
Financial Reports — Understanding Your Business
3:30 – 5:00
Action: Navigate to FC Report from dashboard.

The FC Report — Food Cost Report — is your most important financial health check as an owner. It is only available to you.

It shows you what percentage of your revenue is going back into buying stock. In butchery, a healthy food cost is typically below 35 percent. If your food cost is higher than that, it means either your buying prices are too high, your selling prices are too low, or stock is being wasted or going missing.

Action: Navigate to Reports → Order Reports → Best Sellers tab.

In the Order Reports section, the Best Sellers tab shows you exactly which products are generating the most revenue over any date range you choose. Use this to decide what to buy more of, what to promote, and what is not worth keeping on your shelves.

Action: Click the Restock Suggestions tab. Show the red Critical items.

The Restock Suggestions tab takes your best sellers and compares their sales velocity — how fast they are selling per day — against your current stock levels. It then tells you how many days of stock you have left for each product.

Products in red are critical — they will run out within three days. Products in yellow are low — order within the week. This report should guide your purchasing decisions every single day.

Action: Navigate to Reports → Cashup Reports. Show the variance column.

In the Cashup Reports, you can see every cashup that has been done and the variance for each one. If you are seeing consistent negative variances from the same cashier — meaning they are always short — that is a pattern you need to address.

6
Butchery Carcass Breakdown — Yield & Profit
5:00 – 6:10
Action: Navigate to Butchery Cuts → + New Record.

If your butchery processes whole carcasses in-house — breaking down a whole beef, pork, or lamb into individual cuts — the Butchery Cuts module tracks exactly what you get out of each carcass and whether you are making money from it.

Press New Record to start.

Action: Select a raw product, enter weight and cost price per kg.

Select the raw material — for example, whole beef carcass — enter how many kilograms you are processing, and the cost price per kilogram you paid the supplier. This is your total carcass cost.

Action: Add a few cuts with weight and selling price per kg.

Now add each cut you get out of the carcass — the steak, ribs, mince, brisket — with the weight of each cut and the selling price per kilogram. As you add cuts, you will see the yield percentage building up on the right side.

Show: The P&L summary — gross profit, margin %.

The system automatically calculates your gross profit for this carcass — what you expect to earn from selling all the cuts, minus what you paid for the whole carcass. It also shows your margin percentage and the value of any wastage.

This tells you exactly whether your pricing is right. If your margin is below what you expect, either your cost price is too high or your selling prices need to go up.

7
Stock Manufacturing — Wors, Mince & More
6:10 – 7:00
Action: Navigate to Manufacturing → + New Batch.

If your butchery makes products in-house — like boerewors, seasoned mince, or pap — use the Manufacturing module to record every batch. This tracks the raw materials that go in, the finished product that comes out, and the profit on each batch.

Press New Batch.

Action: Name the output product (e.g. Boerewors), enter qty and selling price.

Name the product you are making, enter how many kilograms you are producing, and the selling price per kilogram. You can also link it to an existing product in your inventory so stock updates automatically.

Action: Add input materials — beef mince, spices, etc.

Now add your input materials — the beef, spices, casing — selecting each from inventory and entering how much you are using. The system deducts these from your stock and calculates the total input cost.

Show: P&L preview — output value vs input cost, gross profit.

The profit and loss preview shows you exactly how much you are making per batch. This is very useful for pricing — if your boerewors is not making enough margin, you can see immediately whether the issue is your raw material cost or your selling price.

8
Stock Count — Owner Controls
7:00 – 7:45
Action: Navigate to Stock Count. Open a completed count.

Stock counts are done by your managers and staff — but as the owner, you have special powers over them.

When you open a completed count report, you see the full variance detail — the difference between what was counted and what the system expected, and the value of any shortages in Rands. Staff cannot see this during the counting process.

Show: The Apply to Stock and Delete Count buttons.

You can apply the count to update live stock levels, or delete the entire count if it was done incorrectly and needs to be redone. Both actions require your owner password to confirm. Deleted counts cannot be recovered, so be certain before deleting.

Only you can apply or delete stock counts. This prevents staff from manipulating stock levels.
9
Online Orders — Setting Up for Customers
7:45 – 8:45
Action: Go to Online Orders → Settings tab.

The Online Orders module lets customers place orders via WhatsApp. To set it up, go to Online Orders and tap the Settings tab.

Action: Show the online/offline toggle. Toggle it on.

This toggle controls whether your store is accepting online orders. When it is green and set to Online, customers can see that you are open for orders. When it is red and Offline, it tells them you are not taking orders right now. Toggle this at the start and end of each day.

Show: The customer order link. Press Copy Link.

Further down, you will see your customer order link. This is a WhatsApp link that, when tapped, opens a WhatsApp chat with a pre-filled "I'd like to order" message. Copy this link and share it on your WhatsApp status, your Facebook page, or even print it as a QR code at your counter. Customers tap it, type their order, and it comes straight through to your Online Orders queue.

Show: The internet speed test button. Run it.

You can also run an internet speed test from here to make sure your connection is good enough for the system to work smoothly.

10
Closing — Summary
8:45 – 9:30
Show: Owner dashboard with all tiles.

Use the financial reports — especially Best Sellers and Restock Suggestions — to make better buying decisions. And use the Carcass Breakdown and Manufacturing modules to understand your real cost and profit on every product you process in-house.

ShopFlow POS is designed to give you complete visibility and control over your business. If you ever need a refresher on any feature, the Help and Training Guide is built right into the app — tap the Help tile on your dashboard at any time.

Thank you for watching, and welcome to ShopFlow POS — powered by ZeHuWo Pty Ltd.
